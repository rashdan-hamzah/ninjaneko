Consulting Problem Solver
01 — Objective

State the client’s core objective in one sentence.

Make it outcome-focused and time-bound (SMART).

Tip: Use a verb + metric + timeline + constraint format.

Sample answer

Increase annual recurring revenue (ARR) by 20% within the next 12 months while keeping gross margin above 65% and NPS above 50.
  • Clear metric (ARR +20%)
  • Time-bound (12 months)
  • Constraints (margin, NPS)
Objective clarity matters.
02 — Profitability

Profits are down. Outline a MECE diagnostic plan.

Structure a revenue/cost tree and hypotheses.

Be hypothesis-led, data-backed, and time-boxed.

Sample answer

Revenue: Price (discounting, mix, promos), Volume (acquisition funnel by channel, conversion, churn).
Cost: COGS (input inflation, waste), OPEX (sales productivity, marketing ROI, support workload).
Prioritize by impact x ease; run cohort analyses for churn; decompose CAC and LTV.
  • Tree is MECE
  • Explicit hypotheses per branch
  • Prioritization framework
Aim for quick wins + root-cause.
03 — Market Sizing

Guesstimate annual coffee cups consumed in the US.

State assumptions and compute.

Top-down or bottom-up; keep numbers round.

Sample answer

Population ~330M; 70% drink coffee → 231M. Avg 1.2 cups/day → ~420 cups/year. 231M × 420 ≈ 97B cups/year.
  • Assumptions stated
  • Simple multiplication
  • Sensitivity: ±10–20%
Round numbers → faster math.
04 — Break-even

Calculate break-even units.

Fixed cost $2M, price $50, variable cost $20.

Contribution margin per unit = price − variable.

Sample answer

BE units = 2,000,000 / (50 − 20) = 66,666.7 ≈ 66,667 units.
  • Contribution margin = $30
  • Interpretation: need ~66.7k units to cover fixed costs
Always sanity-check magnitude.
05 — Pricing

Outline a pricing strategy for a B2B SaaS tool.

Include value metrics and packaging.

Anchor, good-better-best, guardrails.

Sample answer

Value metric: seats + tracked projects. Tiers: Starter ($), Growth ($$), Enterprise ($$$) with SSO, audit logs, premium support. Annual 15% discount; usage-based overages; regional price fences.
  • Value-based metric
  • 3–4 SKUs max
  • Clear fences and add-ons
Price follows value realized.
06 — Prioritization

Pick 2 of 5 initiatives using Impact x Effort.

Explain trade-offs.

Be explicit on impact, risk, and time-to-value.

Sample answer

Choose Referral (H/L) and Onboarding (M/M) for near-term lift and compounding effects. Defer Data Warehouse Migration (H/H) pending resourcing; park Brand Refresh (L/M).
  • Uses a simple scorecard
  • Balancing quick wins with foundations
  • States what not to do
Saying no is strategy.
07 — Root Cause

Churn spiked in APAC by 3 pts. Diagnose.

Frame leading hypotheses and tests.

Combine quant cohorts with qual calls.

Sample answer

Hypotheses: payment failures post price rise; localized latency; competitor promo. Tests: failed payments % trend, RUM metrics, win-loss, NPS themes. Actions: grace periods, APAC CDN POP, promo parity.
  • 3 hypotheses
  • Evidence and quick mitigations
  • Owner + timeline
Define success metrics early.
08 — Growth Levers

Name 3 growth levers for a marketplace.

Briefly justify each.

Think liquidity: match rate, time to match, fill rate.

Sample answer

1) Supply density in top geos to improve fill rate. 2) Repeat demand via subscriptions and reactivation CRM. 3) Trust & safety to reduce multi-homing and increase conversion.
  • Targets marketplace constraints
  • Metrics: match time, fill rate
  • Expect spillover effects
Design for network effects.
09 — KPI Design

Draft a KPI dashboard for a freemium app.

Define north star, input metrics, and guardrails.

Link inputs → outcomes. Avoid vanity metrics.

Sample answer

North star: Weekly Active Teams. Inputs: new team creations, activation rate, day-7 retention, collab actions/week. Monetization: upgrade rate, ARPPU. Guardrails: p95 latency, crash free %, abuse reports.
  • One north star metric
  • Few causal inputs
  • Quality guardrails
Make it actionable.
10 — Recommendation

Give a 60-second executive recommendation (SCQA).

Situation, Complication, Question, Answer.

Lead with the answer; support with 2–3 bullets.

Sample answer

S: Growth slowed post price rise. C: Churn up 3 pts in APAC; conversion down 8% in SMB. Q: How to restore growth in 2Q?
A: Roll back APAC price 5% + add local CDN; launch referral + onboarding refinements. Expect +10% net growth in 90 days.
  • 2–3 crisp actions
  • Timeline and ownership
  • Risks + mitigations
Lead with the answer.
